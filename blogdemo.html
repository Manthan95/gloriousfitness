This workout is perfect for those who have been consistently training for 10-12 weeks and are ready to take their routine to the next level. Whether you're just stepping up from a beginner's stage or already at an intermediate level, these variations will help you achieve a well-rounded upper body. Make sure to warm up properly, maintain proper form, and challenge yourself! If you enjoy the workout, don't forget to like, subscribe, and hit the notification bell to stay updated with more fitness tips and workouts.

Day 1 is a full-body workout starting with 5-10 minutes of light cardio like brisk walking, jogging, or cycling. The workout includes bodyweight squats, push-ups (or knee push-ups), bent-over dumbbell rows, planks, and standing dumbbell presses, each performed in 3 sets of 8-15 reps (or seconds for planks). Afterward, 5-10 minutes of stretching will help cool down and improve flexibility. The focus is on proper form, using light weights or bodyweight initially, and gradually increasing resistance. Adequate hydration, nutrition, and listening to your body are important to avoid injury and support recovery.

Day 3 targets the lower body, starting with a 5-10 minute light cardio warm-up. The workout consists of lunges for leg strength, glute bridges to engage the hips and glutes, calf raises for lower leg development, and leg presses if available. Bodyweight squats are included to further work the legs and glutes, with each exercise performed in 3 sets of 10-20 repetitions. The session ends with a 5-10 minute cool down, focusing on stretching to enhance flexibility and aid recovery.

Day 4 focuses on strengthening the upper body, beginning with a 5-10 minute light cardio warm-up. The workout includes push-ups or knee push-ups for chest strength, followed by dumbbell chest presses and bent-over dumbbell rows to target the chest and back. Bicep curls and tricep dips are included to tone the arms, with each exercise performed in 3 sets of 8-15 repetitions. The session concludes with a 5-10 minute cool down of stretching to promote flexibility and recovery.

Day 6 is another full-body workout that begins with a 5-10 minute light cardio warm-up. The exercises include bodyweight squats, push-ups (or knee push-ups), dumbbell deadlifts, planks, and standing dumbbell presses, all performed in 3 sets of 8-15 reps (or seconds for planks). A 5-10 minute stretching session follows to cool down. Day 7 is dedicated to active recovery, involving 30-45 minutes of light cardio such as walking or cycling, along with stretching or yoga. The plan emphasizes proper form, gradual progression in resistance, and the importance of hydration, nutrition, and listening to your body for rest.
